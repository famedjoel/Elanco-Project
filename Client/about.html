Mission Statement

Elanco is committed to using its animal health expertise to build a sustainable, healthier world for animals and people.

Company History

  • 1954: In 1953, Lilly launched the first antibiotic for exclusive veterinary use. In 1954, the company formally organized its plant and animal science activities into the Agricultural and Industrial Sales Division, led by George Varnes. The Elanco brand, short for Eli Lilly and Company, came to be in 1960, following the reorganization of Eli Lilly and Company's Agricultural and Industrial Sales Division.
  • 1960: The Elanco brand was established following the reorganization of Eli Lilly and Company's Agricultural and Industrial Sales Division.
  • 2007: Elanco acquired Ivy Animal Health, a leader in the development, registration, and marketing of animal health products in Canada.
  • 2015: Elanco acquired Novartis Animal Health, expanding its companion animal portfolio and global reach.
  • 2018: Elanco became a publicly traded company on the New York Stock Exchange.
  • 2020: Elanco acquired Bayer Animal Health, creating the second-largest animal health company by revenue. The acquisition expanded Elanco's portfolio and capabilities, positioning the company for long-term growth.
  • 2021: In August 2021, Elanco announced it had closed its acquisition of Kindred Biosciences, enhancing its existing biologics and monoclonal antibody pipeline. The business plans to break ground on the new headquarters in the first half of 2021.

About Elanco Apps

Elanco Apps is a platform designed to display a data resource group, application name/service name, and various tools related to trend analysis.

The application provides a comprehensive dashboard where users can visualize trends related to resource groups and application names or service names.

Additionally, Elanco Apps includes a dedicated section called the "Tools Dashboard" that offers tools for advanced analysis and insights.
